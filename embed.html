01 — Why now

Putting data on your website is harder than it should be.

You either ship a screenshot that goes stale the day you publish, or you ask engineering for two weeks to build a chart that looks good on mobile.

Screenshots

PNGs of charts on a website is not 2026.

They go stale the day you publish. They look terrible on retina. They can't be hovered, sorted, or filtered. Your "by the numbers" page is the worst section of your site and you know it.

Custom builds

Engineering won't build you another D3 chart.

And if they do, it'll be 3 weeks, then 4 follow-ups when the data shifts, and nobody touches it after the launch. The right team doesn't own the chart; you do.

BI embeds

Tableau Public looks like Tableau Public.

And Looker requires a login. Neither was designed for the front of your site. Your visitors should see something that looks like the rest of your brand — not a 2014 dashboard.

06 — FAQ

Quick answers

How do I put it on my site? ▾
Copy the iframe snippet from the publish screen, paste it into your CMS or HTML. Works in WordPress, Webflow, Notion, Ghost, Squarespace, hand-rolled HTML — anywhere you can drop an iframe.
Can I update the data later? ▾
Yes. Re-upload the CSV, hit publish, every embed of that report updates. No redeploy of your website, no new iframe URL.
Will it match my brand? ▾
Tune colors, typography, and presentation to match your site. On paid plans Vetori chrome is removed entirely. The embed will look native — not like an external dashboard tool.
Is it responsive on mobile? ▾
Yes. The embed reflows for phones and tablets out of the box. No CSS hacks, no separate mobile version.
What if I need it private later? ▾
Switch to Vetori for executive reporting — same product family, recipient-controlled access, audit trail, AI-drafted narratives. Use both side by side; same data, different surface.
How does it compare to Tableau Public or Datawrapper? ▾
Datawrapper is great for journalism static charts. Tableau Public looks like Tableau. Vetori is built to look native to *your* site — not someone else's dashboard tool — and the same artifact can switch from public embed to private branded report when the audience changes.
